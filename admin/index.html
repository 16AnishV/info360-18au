To dos

At least two weeks before class starts

  • Review all course revision ideas and generate action items for each
  • Address any outstanding issues with the Design Methods book
  • Schedule meeting with TA to discuss course

Before class starts

  • Update dates in syllabus based on UW Academic Calendar
  • Update any travel dates on syllabus
  • Update the peer evaluation survey link
  • Update the Google Drive video upload link
  • Find TA or guests to cover any travel dates
  • With TA:
    • Discuss class learning objectives: design thinking skills
    • Discuss pedagogical approach and rationale: reading > lecturing, activities > exams
    • Discuss our undergraduates: tech fluent, occasionally code-phobic
    • Collaboratively decide on a project scope
    • Ask the TA to teach lab in weeks 1-5
    • Ask the TA to come to all classes in weeks 6-10 to provide feedback
    • Schedule a 3-hour grading meeting for Friday of finals
    • Ask TA when they'll be traveling this quarter
    • Ask TA to review syllabus for ambiguity
    • Warn TA about expecting student emails about grades
    • Setup a Slack channel to discuss coordinate with TA

First week of class:

  • Once roster stabilizes, print enough signature sheets for the quarter
  • Once roster stabilizes, print headshots of roster
  • Memorize names (instructor and TA)

Weekly recurring for the first five weeks of class, one week before first class:

  • Inspect and verify appropriateness of reading prompts for reading quizzes
  • Review the required readings to be prepared for discussion
  • Notify TA which classes they need to attend
  • Grade required readings within 1 week of submission (TA)
  • Grade selected readings within 1 week of submission (TA)
  • Grade activity attendance within 1 week of submission (TA)

Week 4

  • Ask TA to print midterm (TA)

Week 6

  • Ask TA to grade midterm (TA)
  • Ask TA to create teams in Canvas
  • Ask TA task to create a new survey for peer evaluation using team names (TA)

Weeks 9 and 10

  • Provide feedback on design specifications (instructor and TA)
    • Identify issues in the same way it will be graded
    • Provide detailed constructive feedback for every issue

Finals week

  • Grade specifications before grading meeting (instructor and TA)
    • Carefully familiarize yourself with the rubric on the syllabus. I like to keep it open while grading so I can refer to the rules.
    • I find it easiest to print the PDFs, rather than using a PDF annotation tool. It's just faster.
    • When you see a violation, write the name of the violation from the rubric (e.g., SUPPORT, LOGIC, RATIONALE) and write a few words to convey why it's a violation. I'll use this to explain to students why they lost the points. (I make them come to my office if they want an explanation). You don't need to provide feedback like you did this past week.
    • Use colored ink to make them easy to find.
    • After finishing one, add up all the points they lost and put a total on the front.
    • Make note of any violations you were unsure about and we'll discuss them on Friday.
    • Bring the annotated printouts to the grading meeting as your ground truth.
  • Grading meeting
    • Reflect on class with TA
    • Look for anomalies in all grades
    • Resolve ambiguities in specification grading rubric
    • Review medians and ranges in specification
    • Post specification scores in Canvas and write feedback detailing why points were lost
    • Notify students of posted grades after all grades are posted
    • With TA, and using peer evaluations, review and grade video prototypes
      • Open the peer evaluation
      • Watch the video
      • Debate each of the parts of the rubric, converging toward scores
    • Ask students for permission to share top specifications and videos in future quarters
    • Unmute all assignments
    • Notify students of grades and that they have the weekend to dispute data entry errors

Monday after finals

  • Compute final percentages from Canvas
  • Export to Excel translate percentages into GPA using formula on syllabus
  • Submit final grades on gradepage
