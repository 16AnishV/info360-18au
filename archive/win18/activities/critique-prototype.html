Critique your prototype

  1. Prepare your prototype to share
  2. Outline a brief script for how you're going to introduce the design to your representative user.
    • How will you introduce the idea?
    • What questions will you ask about it?
    • What kind of feedback are you looking for?
  3. Get feedback from a representative user.
  4. Discuss your feedback with your partner.
    • Do you think your user understood your idea?
    • Would your idea substantively address a problem they have?
    • Are there ways to improve your idea to more directly and effectively address the problem you chose?
  5. If necessary, reconsider your design again, refining your concept or choosing a new one altogether.
  6. Once you've committed to a concept, enumerate all of the mockups you need to communicate every detail of your design. We'll use these in class next week to run an walkthrough.
    • What are all of the parts of the design a user would encounter?
    • What are all of the inputs and outputs that are possible?
    • What is your plan for creating all of these screens? Who will make them and with what tools?
