Plan your interview

The first step in your design process will be to interview the members of your sister team to discover problems in their lives that you can address through design.

In the rest of class, you should plan your interview, probing into the hard problems in your classmates' lives. Your interview should include the following:

  • Establish rapport. How will you get your informant comfortable sharing?
  • Describe your goals. Consent your informant into the purpose of the project and what you'd like to learn. Tell them how you'll be collecting data.
  • Your interview questions. What kinds of problems do you want to learn about? What can you ask to learn about them?
  • Debrief. Thank your participant and tell them how you'll be following up, if at all.

All of the above details should be written down, in advance, to help structure your interview time. You must be ready to conduct your interview by lab time.
